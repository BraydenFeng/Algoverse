Her brother had said the thing again. He had said it differently this time, with a small laugh attached, the way he always attached a small laugh to the things he wanted to say without consequence, and Margery felt the fork in her hand go still over her plate. The roast steamed. Her mother was looking down. Her father was looking at her brother with the small careful no-comment look he had been doing for forty-three years.

"It was a joke," her brother said.

"It wasn't a joke."

"Margery."

"It wasn't a joke. You said the same thing in 2019 and you said the same thing in 2014 and you said the same thing the Christmas before that. It is not a joke. It has never been a joke. You think it is a joke because you have always been allowed to think it is a joke."

"Margery, please." Her mother now. Her mother's hand was on the table.

"No." Margery did not raise her voice. She did not need to raise her voice. She put the fork down. She placed it across her plate the way she had been taught as a child to place a fork across a plate when she was finished, and the fork made the small ceramic click, and the click sat in the room.

Her brother had the small open face he wore when he was about to call her difficult. She watched the face arrange itself. She had been watching the face do this since she had been fourteen. He took a breath. She let him take it. She watched him.

"You always do this," he said.

"I do not always do this," she said. "I never do this. That is the problem. I have never, until tonight, done this, and you have, for years, told yourself that I was fine with it because I did not do this, and now I am doing it, and you are going to have to find a new story to tell yourself about why this is happening, because the story you are about to reach for, the story that this is about me being difficult, is not going to work tonight."

He looked at her. He did not, for a moment, have a face. She saw the face he had under the face. She did not feel sorry for him. She had spent enough years feeling sorry for him.

She picked up her fork. She did not eat. She held it. The roast steamed.
