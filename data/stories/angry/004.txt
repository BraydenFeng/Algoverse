The phone had rung and the name on the screen had been a name Audrey had not seen in four years and she had answered it the way she answered all unknown numbers, with a flat hello, because she did not give the satisfaction of recognition. The voice on the other end had said, hey, it's me, in the small expectant way that assumed the me was self-evident.

"Yes," Audrey said. "I know."

"It's been a minute," her old friend said. The old friend's voice had the small careful brightness of a person who had decided, in advance, to be the bigger person in this call.

"It has."

"I was thinking about you. I — I wanted to reach out."

"Okay."

There was a pause. Audrey did not fill it. She had spent the four years since the falling-out learning, with some effort, to not be the person who filled pauses for other people, and she was not going to abandon that practice on this call.

"Look," the old friend said, "I know things ended badly between us. I know that. I have done a lot of thinking about it. I don't want to get into the whole — but I just wanted to say, I have grown a lot, and I wanted to reach out, and see if you would be open to grabbing coffee sometime."

Audrey held the phone. She felt, very precisely, the heat at the back of her teeth.

"I have done a lot of thinking too," Audrey said evenly. "And what I have decided, in four years of thinking, is that you spoke about my private life to people who used it against me at work, that you knew you were doing it at the time, that you have not, in this phone call, acknowledged any of that, and that you are calling me because you have decided you have grown, not because you have any specific apology for any specific thing you actually did to me."

The old friend exhaled. "I am — I am not saying I didn't — "

"You are exactly saying you didn't. You called and you said you had grown. That is not an apology. That is a press release."

The old friend was quiet. The quiet went on for a few seconds. The quiet went on the way a person waits for the other person to soften.

"I do not need coffee," Audrey said. "I am okay. Take care of yourself."

She ended the call. She set the phone face-down on the counter. Her hands were not shaking. She was surprised. She had expected her hands to shake.
