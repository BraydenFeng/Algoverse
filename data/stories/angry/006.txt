The power had gone out at seven and Beatrice had gone immediately to the utility company's outage map on her phone and the outage map had shown her block as not affected. The map had shown the block to the north as affected and the block to the south as affected and her block, somehow, in some flat green color, as not affected. She had refreshed the page twice. The flat green did not change. The apartment was dark. The fridge had stopped humming. The flat green stayed green.

She called the outage line. She got the menu. She pressed the option for current outages. The recording told her there were no outages reported in her area. She pressed the option to speak to a representative. The recording told her hold times were over forty-five minutes. She did not hang up. She lit a candle one-handed. She set the candle in a small dish on the counter. She paced. The hold music played the same eight bars she had heard the last time she had called this number, which had been three years ago, also about a power outage that the map had told her was not happening.

She thought about how the utility had raised its rates twice in the last year. She thought about how the utility's CEO had been quoted, in a press release the utility had emailed to her in November, as being "deeply committed to customer experience." She thought about the phrase deeply committed. She thought about the word deeply. She paced.

A representative came on the line. The representative was a young man with a calm voice. The representative asked for her account number. She gave it. The representative asked for her address. She gave it. The representative paused and said, "I see no outage at your address, ma'am."

"Yes," Beatrice said. "That is what your map says also. The map is wrong. I am sitting in the dark. The block to the north is out. The block to the south is out. My block is also out. The map says it is not."

"I understand. Let me see if I can — "

"I do not need you to see if you can. I need you to send a truck. The map is wrong. The map has been wrong before. I have called about the map being wrong before. The map needs to be updated and the truck needs to come. Those are the two things I need from you."

The representative was quiet for a beat. The representative had been trained, she could tell, in beats. The representative began the script. She let him say one sentence. She did not let him say two.
