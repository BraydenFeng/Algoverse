The hotel room had been promised with a view of the harbor and the hotel room looked, instead, into a brick airshaft, and Magnus had called the front desk and the front desk had told him that, regrettably, the harbor-view rooms were all occupied and they could move him tomorrow if a guest checked out as expected. The desk had said the word regrettably with the small bright training the chain gave its staff in saying the word regrettably.

Magnus had paid, on the booking site, the harbor-view rate. He had paid, specifically, the upgrade. He had taken a screenshot of the confirmation. He had taken a screenshot, because he had been on this kind of trip enough times to know to take a screenshot. He pulled up the screenshot on his phone. He looked at it. He looked at the brick airshaft. He looked at the screenshot.

He went down to the lobby. The young woman at the front desk gave him the bright trained smile. He did not return it. He set his phone on the marble counter, with the screenshot up, and turned it to face her.

"I would like the room I paid for," he said.

She looked at the phone. She tapped at her terminal. She made the small face the staff was trained to make at the terminal, which Magnus understood from prior trips meant the terminal was being used as a prop while the staff member calculated the next sentence. The next sentence arrived. "Sir, I am so sorry for the inconvenience. As I mentioned to my colleague, all of our harbor-view rooms are currently — "

"I do not care which rooms are currently occupied. I paid for one. The rate I paid is on the screen. The page I booked from said the upgrade was guaranteed. Guaranteed is a particular word. It is your word, not mine. I did not write it on the website. I read it. I clicked the box. I gave you the money."

"Sir."

"Please get your manager."

She paused. The pause was the small calculated pause of staff who had been instructed to attempt three de-escalating moves before escalating. He could see her calculate the move. He watched her calculate it.

"Please," he said again, evenly. "Your manager."

She picked up the phone. She dialed. She spoke quietly. She set the phone down. She gave him the small trained smile. "He will be right with you."

Magnus did not return the smile. He stood at the counter with his hands flat on the marble. He waited.
