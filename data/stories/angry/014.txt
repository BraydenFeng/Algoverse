The doctor had been forty-five minutes late to a fifteen-minute appointment and the doctor had walked in finally and had not apologized, had simply opened the laptop and said, so, what brings you in, and Mara had watched him not look at her chart, the same chart she had described to him in detail at the appointment three weeks ago, which had been a chart with a result on it that had been the entire reason she was sitting in this room.

"You called me in," she said.

"Right, right." He scrolled. He scrolled with the small frown of a man who had been about to look at the chart anyway. He clicked. He scrolled. He read silently for a moment. "Right. So your bloodwork came back — "

"You called me at two o'clock yesterday and asked me to come in today. I left a meeting to come in today. I have been in your waiting room since one-fifteen. It is now two o'clock. I am asking you, before you read me my own bloodwork, to acknowledge that I have been waiting in your waiting room for forty-five minutes for an appointment that you asked me to come in for."

He looked at her. His face did the small adjustment. "I apologize for the delay, Ms. Hadid. We had an emergency."

"Forty-five minutes ago you had an emergency. Twenty minutes ago you had an emergency. What was the emergency."

He paused. "I am not able to discuss other patients."

"I am not asking you to discuss another patient. I am asking you whether you, personally, were in a clinical emergency in this building for the last forty-five minutes, or whether you were running behind, which is a different thing, and which would require, from you, a different kind of acknowledgment."

He set down the laptop. He folded his hands. He had the small careful face of a man who had not, in some time, been asked a direct question by a patient. He said, after a moment, "I have been running behind today."

"Thank you," Mara said.

"I apologize for that."

"Thank you."

He waited. She waited. The silence sat. He picked the laptop back up.

"Your bloodwork," he said.

"Yes."

He told her. She listened. She listened with her hand on the strap of her bag and her feet flat on the floor. When he finished, she asked the three questions she had written down the night before. He answered them. He answered them carefully, this time. He did not, again, scroll past her chart.
