By seven the office was empty and Pria stayed because she had work to redo. Not work that needed redoing because of any failure of hers. Work that needed redoing because the senior partner had, in the meeting at three, taken her draft, which she had circulated two days ago, and had presented the central idea to the client as his own, and the client had liked the idea, and the senior partner had not, while he was presenting, looked at her once, and she had sat in the meeting with her hand on her notebook and had watched the small theft happen across the conference table and had said nothing.

She had said nothing because she had been told, in March, by the same senior partner, that she was perceived as combative. The note had been delivered in her performance review. The note had been delivered the way these notes were delivered, with the small concerned phrasing of a man who was, he wanted her to know, on her side. The note had said her work was excellent. The note had also said that some colleagues had found her tone in meetings, on occasion, sharp. The note had not named the colleagues. The note had not given examples. The note had said, in a soft summary line, that she might consider how she came across.

She had considered it for the eight months since. She had considered it in the meeting today. She had considered it while watching the man across the table take her idea. She had considered it on the elevator down to grab a coffee at four, and on the elevator back up, and at her desk, and at the meeting at five, and at her desk again now.

She had decided, somewhere on the elevator at four, that she was going to stop considering it.

The deciding had been the easy part. The deciding had taken thirty seconds in a small steel box between floors. The doing was the part she was sitting with at seven o'clock in the empty office. The doing would mean a meeting. The doing would mean naming, in the meeting, a specific thing that the senior partner had done. The doing would mean using the word in a sentence with a clear subject and a clear verb. The doing would mean being, in his report on her in next March's review, more than perceived as combative.

She turned to her keyboard. She opened the draft. She did not redo it. She started writing the meeting in her head. She wrote it carefully. She did not let herself write a single sentence she would not say.
