The driver in the silver sedan in front of Wendel had braked for no reason for the third time in two miles and Wendel had been keeping a fine distance and the braking had still made him hit his own brakes harder than he had wanted to. The coffee in the cup holder had jumped and a dribble had gone down the side. He had said, aloud, in the empty car, a word he did not say.

The silver sedan was going forty-eight in a sixty-five and was in the left lane. The left lane was the passing lane. Wendel had been signaling for two miles to suggest, in the small visible way one driver suggests to another, that perhaps the silver sedan might consider getting over. The silver sedan had not considered.

He could see the driver in the silver sedan in the side mirror. The driver was on the phone. The driver was talking with the small animated gestures of a person who had forgotten where he was. Wendel watched the gestures. Wendel watched the gestures and felt the small specific anger that was the anger of a person being held up, on a wide empty highway, by a person who was not, in any sense, paying attention to the highway he was holding up.

Wendel changed lanes to the right. He passed the silver sedan. He kept his eyes forward when he passed. He had wanted to look. He had wanted to look at the driver and let the driver see that he had been looked at. He did not look. He kept his eyes forward. He passed. He pulled back into the left lane two car-lengths ahead and set his cruise control at sixty-eight, which was his usual speed, and which the silver sedan had cost him eleven minutes of.

The silver sedan dropped back. The silver sedan was no longer in his mirror. The road was clear ahead.

The anger did not, however, drop back. The anger stayed with him through the next exit, and through the next mile marker, and through the next fifteen miles, attached to nothing now, attached to a driver who was three minutes behind him on a road Wendel would never see again. He drove. He gripped the wheel a little tighter than he wanted to grip the wheel. He noticed himself gripping. He loosened his hands deliberately. They tightened again on their own. He let them. He drove.
