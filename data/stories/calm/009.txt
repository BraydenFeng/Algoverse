The board said DELAYED and Pär had read it once when he arrived and had not read it since. He had a book. He had a coffee. He had a window seat on the bench facing the platform, which was the seat he would have chosen anyway, delay or no delay. There was no train. There would be a train when there was a train.

He turned the page of the book. The book was a novel he had been carrying around for three weeks and finally had the long stretch of time to make headway with, and he was making headway. The book was good. The chapter he was in was particularly good. The platform around him was nearly empty. A woman at the far end was knitting and not looking at the board. Two men in work coats were sharing a thermos. Nobody was upset about the train.

A pigeon walked past his feet with the small businesslike walk pigeons did when they were looking for crumbs. He had no crumbs. The pigeon looked up at him, accepted this, and moved on. He watched it go.

He read for another twenty minutes and then he set the book face-down on the bench beside him and let his eyes rest. The station's roof was the old kind, iron arches and glass, and a few panes were missing and the cold air came down through the gaps in soft cold ribbons. He pulled his scarf a little higher. The coffee was at the temperature where it was no longer hot and not yet cold, which was the temperature he preferred.

The announcement came on. The voice apologized for the continued delay. The voice gave a new estimate. The new estimate added another hour. Pär nodded to the loudspeaker as if the voice could see him. The men with the thermos passed it between them. The knitting woman did not look up. The pigeon, now down the platform, did not care.

He picked the book back up. He found his place. He read another paragraph and the paragraph was very good and he set the book down again because the paragraph deserved a moment. He drank some coffee. He looked at the empty rails. The light was changing slowly the way light changed in the late afternoon of a winter day, going amber at the edges. He thought, well, this is what I have. This is what I have right now. And it was, and he had it.
