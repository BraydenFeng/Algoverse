The appointment was over and Brent stood in the parking lot with his hand on the door of his car and did not get in yet. The visit had been routine. The doctor had been pleased. His numbers were good. He had been told to keep doing what he had been doing, which was mostly walking, and to come back in six months. He had said he would, and he meant it.

The parking lot was small and the lot abutted a low hedge that abutted a stretch of sidewalk and beyond the sidewalk was a street with not much on it. The afternoon was the kind of afternoon that did not commit to weather. The sun went and came and went again behind a low even cloud. The light on the windshield of his car kept changing.

He had two hours before his wife would get home. He had no errands. He had brought the book he had been meaning to start, which was in the passenger seat. He could go home and read on the porch. He could drive the long way home and stop at the bakery and bring back the small round bread his wife liked. He could sit in the car for a while and just sit.

He took his hand off the door. He sat down on the low concrete curb at the edge of the parking lot. He was wearing the good wool coat and the curb was clean enough. He sat. He watched a pickup truck pass on the street, then a school bus that was empty, then nothing for a while.

He felt his ribs move with breath. He felt, in the small way he had been learning to feel in the last year, that his body was a thing that had had to do work for a long time and was, today, doing it well, and was being thanked. The thanking was a quiet thanking. The thanking did not need words. The thanking was him sitting on this curb in the wool coat and noticing the breath.

A leaf came down across the lot in a slow spiral and landed at his feet. He looked at the leaf. The leaf was yellow with a few small spots. He had seen many leaves in his life. He had not, until just now, seen this one. He looked at it for a long moment. Then he stood up, and dusted off the back of his coat, and opened the car door.

He drove the long way. He stopped at the bakery.
