The hotel was in a city Soren had never been to and the hotel room was on the eighth floor and the eighth floor had a small balcony, and Soren had taken his coffee out to the balcony and was leaning on the rail. The morning was just getting itself organized. The street eight stories down had the soft early traffic of a city that was not in a hurry yet. A cyclist went past slowly. A man with a long broom was sweeping the sidewalk in front of a café.

Soren did not know the name of the street. He had a map of the neighborhood in his bag and he had looked at it last night and he had decided he would walk first and look at the map after, and the decision had felt right and still felt right. He had three days here. He had a list of two things he wanted to see, which he had written on the back of his boarding pass, and he had given himself permission to not see either of them if it turned out he did not feel like it.

The coffee was good. The hotel had a small machine in the room that he had distrusted at first and then accepted. The cup was the small white cup of a hotel room and it sat well in the hand. He drank.

A pigeon landed on the rail two meters down from him and looked at him with the level, unbothered attention of a city pigeon. He looked back. The pigeon adjusted its feet on the rail and decided he was not interesting and looked elsewhere. Soren respected this judgment.

Across the street a window opened on the third floor and a woman leaned out and shook a small rug into the air and the rug let go of its dust and the dust drifted into the sun, and Soren found this very beautiful in a small offhand way that did not require him to do anything about it. He held the cup. He watched the dust. The woman closed the window.

He thought about the day ahead and the day was a shape rather than a list. He would walk. He would find somewhere to eat lunch. He would, at some point, sit down. He would, at some point, get tired and come back. The bed was made up by the cleaners at eleven and he liked coming back to a made bed in the early afternoon. This was the small luxury of a hotel he had learned to enjoy.

He finished the coffee. He stayed at the rail.
