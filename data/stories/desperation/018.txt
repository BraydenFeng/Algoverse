The beach was empty in the way only an off-season beach is empty, the kind of empty that says everyone who has any sense is somewhere else. Wren walked along the wrack line and called the dog's name and the wind took the name and threw it sideways. The dog had bolted from the car at the boardwalk forty minutes ago. The dog was a rescue. The dog had been with her three months. The dog had been working on coming when called. The dog had not, today, come when called.

The tide was going out and the tide was going out fast. Wren did not know this beach. She knew there was a jetty to the north and she knew the cliffs ended a half-mile south at the channel and she knew that between the jetty and the channel there was nothing but sand and water and a few stranded gulls. She had walked the sand twice already. She walked it a third time and the dog was not in the sand. She turned and looked at the water.

The water was gray and lifting and the gulls were going up off it in clusters. She could not see a dog in the water. She could not be sure. She walked into the surf to her ankles and then to her knees and the cold was an animal that climbed her legs and her jeans dragged and she kept going until the next wave caught her at the waist and she stopped because she had to. She stood in the water and called.

"Mira. Mira. Come on. Mira."

The wind. The gulls.

She came back onto the sand. She was shivering. Her phone was in her coat in the car. The car was a quarter-mile away over the dunes. She did not want to leave the beach because the dog might come back to the beach and not find her. She did not want to stay on the beach because she was wet to the waist and the wind was rising and she had been here for forty minutes already and forty minutes was the kind of forty minutes that becomes an hour and then becomes something you tell people about for the rest of your life.

She started walking north along the sand calling the name. The wind kept taking the name. The wind kept taking it. The wind kept taking it. Wren kept calling. The dog had been with her three months. The dog had been the first thing in a long time. The dog had been the first thing.
