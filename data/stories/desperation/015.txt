The toasts had stopped twenty minutes ago and the maid of honor had not yet given hers and the maid of honor was Theo's sister and Theo's sister was in the bathroom and Theo's sister had been in the bathroom for forty-five minutes. Theo had been to the bathroom door three times. Theo had knocked and called her name and asked if she was alright and the answer the third time had been a long silence followed by, "I can't do it. I'm sorry. I cannot do it." The DJ had been improvising. The DJ had now run out of things to improvise. The room was beginning to murmur.

Theo went back to the bathroom door. He set his forehead against the door. He spoke through the door very low.

"Hey. Hey. It's me. It's me. You don't have to do it. Okay? You don't have to. We will skip it. We will say you got food poisoning. Nobody — nobody cares. Listen to me. Nobody cares. We will skip it."

"Theo."

"Listen. The bride. Listen. The bride is — she's going to be okay. She is. I will tell her. I will tell her right now. I will go tell her right now. Just please come out. Please come out."

"I had it. I had it written. I lost the paper. I lost the paper and I cannot remember any of it. I cannot remember any of it. I tried for forty minutes."

"You don't need the paper."

"I need the paper."

"You don't. You don't. You can — you can stand up and you can say one thing. One. The first thing you think of about her. The first thing. That is the speech. That is the whole speech. Okay?"

A pause. He could hear her breathing on the other side of the door. He could hear her breathing the way you breathe when you have been crying and you are trying to stop.

"What if I — what if I cry up there."

"Then you cry up there. People love it when you cry up there. The bride loves it when you cry up there. Please. Please. The bride is looking at me. The bride is looking at me right now. Please come out. Anything. One sentence. We can — we can leave after, we can — "

The lock turned. The door opened a half inch. Theo could not see her face yet but he could see her hand on the door, and the hand was trembling, and the hand was reaching out, and he took the hand.
