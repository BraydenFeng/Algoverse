The next bus was at 11:40 and the bus she had needed had been the one at 10:15 and the one at 10:15 had been the last one that would get her to the courthouse by noon. The board in the bus station had said 10:15 DEPARTED in flat green letters when she had run in at 10:17. She had stood in front of the board with her hand on her chest and her breath in pieces and she had read the word DEPARTED four times like it might rearrange itself into something else.

It was 10:34 now and she had been pacing the same eight feet of tile in front of the ticket counter for seventeen minutes. The clerk had looked at her twice and then stopped looking at her. The clerk had nothing for her. The clerk had told her the schedule was the schedule. The clerk had told her there was no other bus before 11:40 and there was no taxi service that ran out to the courthouse from this station and the next town over had a taxi but the next town over was twenty-five miles and she did not have a car.

She had to be at the courthouse at noon. She had to. If she was not at the courthouse at noon the petition was dismissed without prejudice, which sounded like nothing but was not nothing, was actually everything, was the difference between her son living with her in September and her son not living with her in September. The judge had said this last week, in the calmest possible voice, and the calmness had been a warning, and she had nodded.

She tried Rideshare. She tried Rideshare again. No drivers. She tried Rideshare a third time and lowered her preferences and tried again. No drivers. She walked outside the bus station and held her phone up. She walked back inside. She walked to the door of the bus station and looked at the road. The road was empty. There was a man getting out of a pickup truck on the other side of the road. She watched him. She watched him lock the truck. She watched him walk into a hardware store.

She did not know him. She had eleven dollars in cash. She had a face that she hoped looked like the face of a person you would help, not the face of a person you would not. She was going to ask. She was going to walk over there and she was going to ask. She started across the parking lot.
