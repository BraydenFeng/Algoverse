The board had been showing the same word for an hour and the word was DELAYED. There was no time after the word. No estimate, no platform, no apology, just the flat capital letters and the small red blinking dot beside them. Hugo had checked his watch eleven times since he sat down on the bench, and each time the hands had moved and the board had not. He had to be in the city by nine. He had to be. The contract was at nine. The notary was at nine-fifteen. He had the envelope in his coat. He had the envelope in his coat and if he was not at the table at nine the deal went to the other buyers and the other buyers had been waiting six weeks for him to fail.

He went to the agent's window. The agent at the window was on a call and held up a finger and Hugo waited and then the agent put the call down and looked at him.

"There's no estimate yet, sir."

"I have to be — I have to be in the city by nine. Is there another route. Is there a — is there a coach. Is there a — "

"Coaches are not running. The line is the line."

"Please. Is there anybody. Anybody you can ask. Is there a — a freight, a — anything. I'll pay. I'll pay whatever — "

"Sir."

"Please."

The agent's face did not change. "There is no estimate yet, sir."

Hugo walked back to the bench. He sat down. He stood up. He sat down. He took out his phone and called the buyer and the buyer did not pick up. He called the notary and the notary did not pick up. He called his lawyer and his lawyer picked up and his lawyer said there was nothing his lawyer could do, the contract said in person, and Hugo could hear his own voice rising in the receiver, asking, asking, was there nothing, was there really nothing, what about a video, what about a courier, what about a — and his lawyer said no, no, no, very gently, and the call ended.

The board flickered. He looked up. The board still said DELAYED. The little red dot blinked. The platform was empty. Hugo pressed both hands to his face and tried to think of one person, one, who could fix this, and could not.
