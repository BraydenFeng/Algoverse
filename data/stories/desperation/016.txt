The library had closed at nine and Jonas had heard the lock turn at the front doors at nine-oh-two and he had not moved. He was in the back stacks on the third floor, in the carrel he had been working at since two, and the laptop on the carrel had eleven minutes of battery left and the file on the laptop was not on the cloud. The file on the laptop was forty-one pages of his dissertation. The file on the laptop had not been backed up in nineteen days because Jonas had been telling himself he would back it up tonight, after the library, when he got home.

The screen flickered. The fan, which had been running at full speed for an hour, was now slowing. The laptop did this when it was about to give up. He had two ports. He had no charger. The charger was in his apartment because he had left in a hurry that morning and he had told himself the laptop was at eighty percent and he would be fine. He had not been fine. He had been writing without saving. He had stopped saving at six because saving had triggered some kind of indexing thing and the indexing had been freezing the screen and he had clicked through twelve save dialogs and then he had just stopped saving.

He pressed control-S. The screen blinked. A dialog opened. The dialog asked something he did not have time to read. He pressed enter. The dialog closed. He did not know what he had agreed to. He pressed control-S again. The save indicator turned. The save indicator turned. The save indicator was still turning. The battery icon flashed.

He thought, do not panic. He thought, do not panic. He thought, the laptop is going to die in two minutes and the file is going to die with it and your advisor's deadline is Friday and you do not have a backup of any of the last seven weeks of work.

He looked around the carrel. There was a wall outlet four feet away. He had nothing to put in it. He had no phone charger that would charge a laptop. He thought about the librarian's desk on the second floor. He thought about whether a librarian charger would even fit his laptop. The save indicator turned. The save indicator turned. The save indicator —

The screen went black.

He sat very still in the dark, and the only sound was the building's ventilation, and the only thing he could think was the file, the file, the file.
