Bell had three minutes between classes and the test was in fourth period and she did not know any of it. She did not know the unit. She had thought it was Tuesday and the test was Thursday and the test was today. The textbook was in her locker and her locker was a hallway away and the hallway was filling up. She started running. She did not run in the halls. The hall monitor saw her and she pretended she did not see him and she kept going. She had the textbook open before the lock had finished clicking shut.

She read the first page. The page was words. She read it again. She did not know any of the words. She skipped to the chapter review. Mitosis. Meiosis. Prophase. Metaphase. Anaphase. Telophase. She tried to make the words into pictures. The pictures slid off the words. She tried again. She had nine minutes left of passing period and she had spent six standing at her locker reading words.

Her best friend went past and Bell grabbed her sleeve. "Tell me. Tell me everything. Right now. Mitosis. What is — what does it — "

"Bell."

"Two minutes. Just give me two minutes. Anything. The order. Just the order. I just need to — "

"Bell, the bell is about to — "

"Please. Please please please. I cannot fail this test. I cannot. My mom — please. Just the order."

Her friend looked at her. Her friend exhaled. "Prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase. PMAT. P-M-A-T. Say it back."

"P-M-A-T."

"Again."

"P-M-A-T."

"That's mitosis. Meiosis has two rounds, you do the same letters twice. Got it?"

"Got it. Got it. Wait. What's the difference between — "

The bell rang. The hallway emptied. She was already running. She had the textbook open in front of her and she was running and reading and saying P-M-A-T under her breath like a prayer and she was three doors down from the science room and she had not eaten lunch and her hands were shaking and she had not, even once, in the last ten minutes, stopped to think that the test was going to be graded and the grade was going to her mother and her mother had already said one more bad grade and there would be no phone for the summer. The classroom door was closing. She got her foot in.
