The interviewer had stopped taking notes ten minutes ago, and Marcus had watched the pen go still on the legal pad like a small animal that had given up. He kept talking anyway. He had to keep talking. If he stopped, the silence would do the rest of the work for them, and then they would smile the close-out smile, and then the email would come tomorrow, and then the email would not come at all, and his daughter's tuition deposit was due in eleven days.

He heard himself oversell a project from 2019. He heard the word "passionate" leave his mouth twice in one sentence. He felt the sweat at his collar and could not reach for it without confessing to it. The man across the desk had folded his hands in a way that meant the interview was already over and the man was being polite about it. Marcus tried to bring it back. He tried to find the thread of the answer he had practiced in the car, but the thread had snapped and now he was just pulling at the loose end of it.

"What I think I'm trying to say," he said, and then he could not remember what he had been trying to say. The man waited. The clock on the wall behind the man's head said 10:47. He had thirteen minutes, and after that the receptionist would buzz, and after that he would be in the elevator, and after that he would be in the parking garage with no plan.

He thought about Maya, eleven years old, who had decided last week that she wanted to be a marine biologist. He thought about the brochure on the kitchen counter. He thought about the line of credit he had opened in February that he had not told his wife about. He thought about the unread emails from his last manager, the ones he kept meaning to answer.

"I can do this job," he said, and it came out smaller than he had wanted it to. "I know I can do this job. If you give me — if there's any room to — "

The man uncrossed his hands and set them flat on the desk. "Marcus, I appreciate you coming in."

"Please," Marcus said, and the word was out before he could stop it. "Please. I just need a chance."

The man looked at the legal pad. He did not pick up the pen.
