The hotel room smelled like someone else's perfume and the heater clicked on every nine minutes whether she touched the dial or not. Anya had been on hold with the bank for forty-three minutes. The hold music had cycled through the same eight bars of pan flute six times since she had started counting and she had stopped counting after the sixth because counting was making it worse. The flight home was in five hours. Her card had been frozen at the desk an hour ago when she had tried to check out. The desk had been polite about it. The desk had handed the card back across the marble counter with a face that had nothing in it. She had said she would come back. She had come back to the room.

She did not know the city. She did not speak the language past hello and thank you. The phrasebook was at the bottom of her bag and she did not need a phrasebook, she needed her bank to pick up. She needed her bank to pick up and tell her the card was unfrozen and then she needed to go pay the bill at the desk and then she needed to get in a taxi and then she needed to be at the airport in four and a half hours, and every minute the pan flute looped was a minute her plan got tighter. She did not know what would happen if she missed the flight. The next flight was Sunday. Sunday was three days away. She had eleven of the local currency in her wallet. She had no other card.

She thought about who she could call. Her sister was in another time zone and her sister did not pick up unknown numbers. Her ex-husband would pick up. She would not call him. She would not. She would rather sleep in the lobby than call him. Would she? The pan flute looped. Would she? She felt the answer change inside her in real time and she did not like it and she could not stop it.

A voice came on the line. A real voice. "Thank you for holding. How may I — "

"Hello. Hello, yes, please. My name is — I'm overseas — my card has been frozen and I have a flight — I have a flight in less than five hours and I need to check out of my hotel and I cannot — please. Please. Tell me what to do."

The voice asked her to verify her identity. She got the first three answers right. She blanked on the fourth.
