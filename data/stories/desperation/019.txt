The porch light was on. The porch light had always been on. Min had not stood on this porch in eleven years and the porch light was the same porch light, the same wattage, the same hum, and Min could feel the eleven years pressing on her shoulders like a coat she could not get out of.

She had rehearsed the knock. She had rehearsed the knock in her apartment, in her car, in the rest stop bathroom an hour ago. She had rehearsed the first thing she would say. She had rehearsed three first things. The three first things were on a folded piece of paper in her pocket and the paper was damp from her hand and she had not looked at it for the last forty minutes because she was afraid that if she looked at it once more she would not knock.

She raised her hand. She lowered her hand. She turned around and looked at the driveway. The driveway was the same. The mailbox was the same. The crack in the third paving stone was the same. Her mother had filled the crack with epoxy in 2010 and the epoxy had grayed and the crack had reopened a half inch beside it and Min had not known until just now, looking at the crack, that she had remembered the crack. There was a lot she had not known she remembered.

She raised her hand again. She made herself raise it. She made it stay raised. She thought, you drove eleven hours. You drove eleven hours and you are not going to do this. She thought, you drove eleven hours and you are not going to leave without doing this. She thought, knock. Knock. Knock now.

Her hand would not knock. Her hand hovered an inch from the wood. She could hear the television on inside. She could hear two voices, low. She could hear someone laugh. She could hear that one of the voices was her father's voice and the laugh had been her father's laugh and the laugh had not changed.

She lowered her hand. She felt herself, very small, turn to face the door, and not knock, and not knock, and not knock. The light on the porch hummed. A moth went past her ear. She thought, if I do not do this in the next ten seconds I will not do it. She started counting. She got to four. She got to seven. She got to nine. She raised her hand. She knocked.
