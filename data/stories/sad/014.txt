The appointment had been routine and at the end of it the doctor had paused, in a way Greta had not seen the doctor pause before, and had said, there is one thing I want to mention, and Greta had nodded the way patients nod, and the doctor had told her. The doctor had used a word and the word had not been a dramatic word and the doctor had said it was very treatable, very treatable, almost always, and Greta had nodded and had asked one or two clarifying questions and had thanked the doctor and had walked out to her car.

She was sitting in her car now. The car was in the parking lot. She had not started the engine. She had her hands in her lap and her keys beside her on the passenger seat. The afternoon was overcast. The parking lot was almost empty. A woman with a stroller walked past the front of her car and did not look in.

She thought, very calmly, about her sons. Both of them grown. Both of them, at this hour, at their jobs. She would call them this evening. She would tell them the word the doctor had used and she would tell them what the doctor had said about how very treatable it was. They would react, each in his way. Her older son would ask questions. Her younger son would go quiet and then call back in an hour. She knew them.

She thought about her husband. Her husband had been gone four years. She thought, in the small absurd way these thoughts came, that she was, for the first time, glad he was not here to hear this. He would have been frightened. He would have been frightened more than the situation called for, and his fright would have been a thing she would have had to manage on top of her own. The thought was disloyal and she let it stand anyway. He had not been built for hospital news. She had loved him very much. She let the small disloyal thought sit beside the great love and they did not, somehow, cancel each other.

A drop of rain hit the windshield. Then another. The afternoon was going to do its rain. She turned the key. The wipers came on automatically and swept once. The radio came on at the station it had been on this morning. She turned the radio off. She backed out of the space slowly. She drove home in the gathering rain, paying attention to the road, the way she always paid attention to the road.
