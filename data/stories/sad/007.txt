The bench was cold and Auggie did not mind the cold. He sat with his hands in his coat pockets and his breath coming out in small white clouds and he watched the bare trees and did not think anything in particular. The park was nearly empty. A woman with a stroller went past at the far end of the path. A man walked a small dog. The dog stopped at every third tree and the man waited and Auggie watched them, and they went past, and were gone.

He had been coming to this bench for three weeks. He had come every other day. He had not told anyone he had been coming. The bench had been the bench his grandfather had liked to come to, and his grandfather had died in October, and Auggie had not, at the funeral, been able to cry, and he had felt the not-being-able-to-cry as a small wrongness, and he had been coming to the bench since November to see if the bench would do for him what he had not been able to do for himself. The bench had not. The bench had been kind in the way benches were kind, which was to say it had held him up, and not asked anything of him, and that was all.

A pigeon walked along the edge of the path and stopped and looked at him and walked on. He watched the pigeon. His grandfather had liked pigeons. His grandfather had liked many small ordinary things that the rest of the family had not particularly noticed his grandfather liking, and Auggie had been the only grandchild who had sat at the kitchen table on Saturday mornings while his grandfather drank coffee and pointed at the small ordinary things out the window and named them. Cardinal. Mourning dove. The squirrel he had decided to call Helen for reasons he had never explained.

Auggie thought about Helen the squirrel and his eyes filled and he sat with them filling and he did not, even now, cry. The eyes just filled and stayed filled and he blinked and the tears moved a little and that was all. The trees were bare. The light was the gray light of three in the afternoon in February. He thought, you don't have to cry. You don't have to. The bench is here. You are here. He is not here.

He sat with that. The pigeon came back. He watched it walk past.
