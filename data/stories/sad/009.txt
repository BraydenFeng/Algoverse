The board said DELAYED and Otto had stopped reading it. He sat on a bench at the end of the platform with his suitcase by his foot. The train would come when it came. He had nowhere he had to be by a particular hour. The funeral was tomorrow. The funeral would still be tomorrow whether the train arrived now or in three hours.

He had been on the bench for forty minutes. The platform was thinning. A young woman across the way was on her phone with someone, laughing quietly at something the someone had said, and the laughter sounded, in the late afternoon light of the station, like something from a different country, a country he could see but could not enter.

He thought about his uncle. He thought about how, the last time he had seen his uncle, two summers ago at a cousin's wedding, his uncle had taken him aside in the lobby of the hotel and had said, you should come visit, and he had said he would, and he had not. He had meant it at the time. The meaning it had been, somehow, the part that did not transfer into the actual booking of a flight. He had thought he had years. He had thought everyone in his family had years.

The board flickered and updated. The estimate moved another forty minutes. He nodded at the board the way you nod at a doctor's office that has run late. The young woman on the phone was still laughing. He did not begrudge her the laughter. He could remember being on the phone with someone and laughing like that. He hoped she would have many more years of laughing on phones in train stations.

A man with a small accordion came onto the platform from the far end and began to play, very quietly, a slow song Otto did not recognize. The man was not asking for money. The man had no hat out. The man was just playing. The song was simple and a little sad and Otto sat with it. The young woman across the way put her phone down and listened too. The accordion went on for a few minutes and then stopped and the man slung the strap and walked down the platform and out.

Otto watched the empty rails. The board said DELAYED. He thought about getting up and finding a coffee. He did not get up. He stayed on the bench. The cold settled on the platform. He folded his hands in his lap.
