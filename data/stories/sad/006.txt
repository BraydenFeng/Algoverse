The power had gone out at seven and the apartment was dark and Imogen had not lit the candles. She sat at the kitchen table in the dark with her hands flat on the wood. The fridge had stopped humming and the absence of the hum was the loudest thing in the room. Outside, the whole block was dark, and the dark was the soft dark of a city that had let go of itself for a few hours.

The candles were in the drawer. She knew where they were. She had moved them there in October, three weeks before he left, when they had been preparing for the storm season. He had asked her where she had put them and she had told him and he had nodded. They had not, in the end, lost power that fall. The candles had stayed in the drawer.

She thought about getting up. She did not get up. The kitchen chair was hard under her and the hardness was a thing she was using to know where she was. She let her eyes adjust to the dark and slowly the dark became less dark, and the shapes of the cabinets came forward, and the small white shape of the saltshaker came forward, and the table came forward, and she was sitting at her own table in her own kitchen and the kitchen was, at the moment, exactly her size.

She thought about the time the power had gone out in the third year. They had been making pasta. He had laughed and said, well, the water is hot already, and they had eaten the pasta by candlelight at this table and she had thought, at the time, that she would remember the dinner for the rest of her life, and she had been right, and the remembering had become a thing she did with a different mouth now, a mouth that had said different things to him in the last year, and she did not know how to fit that mouth around the memory anymore.

A cat moved softly along the counter. Her cat. The cat jumped down onto the chair beside her and from the chair onto the table and walked across the table and butted its head against her hand. She put her hand on its back. The cat purred. The cat sat down on the table beside her hand and looked at her in the dark. She let the cat look. She did not turn on a flashlight. She did not light the candles. She sat in the dark with the cat and did not move.
