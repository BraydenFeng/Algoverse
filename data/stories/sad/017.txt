The bus station was warm and Birgit was sitting on the bench by the window and the bench had a clear view of the platform where the buses pulled in. She had a ticket in her coat pocket. The ticket was for the four-fifteen and the four-fifteen was the bus that would take her back to the city, alone, after a week of helping her father move out of the house she had grown up in.

The house was sold. The house had been sold for two months. The closing was tomorrow and her father had moved into the small apartment near her brother's place yesterday and her father was, by now, asleep in the new apartment, in the new bed her brother had assembled, and Birgit had stayed one extra night in the house to finish the final cleaning that the buyers had asked for. The house was clean. The keys were with the lawyer. There was nothing more for her to do.

The bench was vinyl and warm from the radiator beneath it. A young man sat at the far end with earbuds in and was nodding very slightly to whatever he was hearing. An older couple was at the snack counter buying coffee. The clerk behind the snack counter laughed at something the older woman had said.

Birgit looked out the window at the platform. She thought about the kitchen. She thought about the kitchen on Sunday mornings, with her mother at the stove and the radio on the windowsill and her father at the table doing the crossword in pen, and how the kitchen had smelled, and how she had not, the last time she had been in the kitchen, three days ago, thought to stand in it for a long moment and take it in, and how she would not, now, ever stand in it again.

She had taken her mother's salt shaker. The salt shaker was in her bag, wrapped in a dish towel. It was a small green ceramic salt shaker and it had been on the kitchen table for forty years. The new family would not miss it. She had asked her father if he wanted it for the new apartment. He had said no, you take it. She had taken it.

She watched the platform. The four-fifteen pulled in. The doors hissed open. She stood. She walked out into the cold. She found her seat. She sat by the window. The bus pulled away. She watched the station get smaller.
