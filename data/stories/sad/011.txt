The passing bell had rung and Roman was still at his locker. The hallway was emptying around him. He had a test next period and he was not, today, going to walk into the test on time, and the not-walking-in-on-time was the small visible part of a larger thing he was learning to carry. His best friend had moved over the weekend. Not died. Not anything. The family had moved. To a state Roman had been to once on a trip in fifth grade and could not remember.

His friend had cleaned out his own locker on Friday and the locker his friend had used was three down from Roman's and the locker was open and empty now, the small metal door swung wide, the inside of the locker bare except for a single magnet that was still stuck to the wall, a magnet of a band they had liked together in seventh grade. The magnet had clearly been forgotten. The magnet sat there in the empty locker the way things sit in empty places.

Roman looked at it. He had been looking at it for two minutes. The bell had rung sixty seconds ago. He did not move.

His other friends had been kind that morning at breakfast. Someone had sat at his table who did not usually sit at his table, and the sitting had been a kind sitting, and Roman had appreciated it and had not been able to find the right thing to say to it. He had eaten his cereal. He had nodded. He had gotten on the bus.

He thought about texting his friend right now, in front of the empty locker. He thought about saying, hey, you left the magnet. He thought about saying, hey. He did not text. His phone was in his bag. The text would come at the wrong time, in the middle of the school day, and the friend was in a new state now and on a different schedule, and Roman did not, quite, know what his friend's schedule was, and the not-knowing was the part of this he had not, yet, understood would become permanent.

He closed the empty locker. The door clicked. The magnet was, somehow, still inside, behind the closed door, and he could not take the magnet, because the locker was not his. He turned and walked toward the science hallway. The bell to start class was about to ring. He walked into the test thirty seconds late. The teacher looked up and said nothing.
