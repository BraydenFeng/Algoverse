A typical office supply drawer contains a small set of items that support routine paperwork. The composition varies between offices, but the same general categories appear in most desks. The arrangement of items within the drawer is usually informal, and individual workers reorganize their drawers to suit their habits.

Writing implements form the largest category. Ballpoint pens, often in black and blue, are the most common; some workers also keep red pens for marking, and a few keep mechanical pencils or wooden pencils. The pens are commonly stored loose in a corner of the drawer, sometimes in a small tray with compartments. Worn-out pens accumulate among the working ones; periodic cleaning of the drawer removes them.

Highlighters appear in a small number of colors, typically yellow, pink, green, and blue. They are used for marking documents during review. Permanent markers are kept less often; their solvent smell makes them better suited to a separate location.

A small stapler and a box of staples are kept near the front of the drawer for easy access. The stapler is usually a basic desktop model that handles up to twenty-five sheets. A staple remover, shaped like a small pair of pincers, is kept beside the stapler. A box of paper clips, typically in a small magnetic dispenser that keeps the clips upright, sits near the stapler.

Adhesives form another category. A roll of clear tape, mounted in a small weighted dispenser, is often kept toward the right side of the drawer where the user can reach it without looking. A bottle of correction fluid or a strip of correction tape sits among the writing implements. A glue stick may be present in offices that prepare physical documents for mounting or filing.

Cutting tools are typically limited to a pair of scissors and possibly a small utility knife. The scissors are placed blade-down in a corner of the drawer or in a small sheath.

Smaller miscellaneous items fill the remaining space. Sticky notes in several sizes are stacked in a corner. A small ruler, often six inches long, lies flat along one side. A handful of rubber bands occupies a small container. A box of small binder clips sits beside the paper clips.

The drawer is usually closed firmly at the end of the day. Some workers keep a small spare key inside.
