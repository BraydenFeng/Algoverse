A peanut butter sandwich is a simple cold preparation made from two slices of bread and a spread of peanut butter. The standard procedure requires few tools and produces a portable, calorie-dense food that keeps well at room temperature for several hours.

The required materials are two slices of bread, a quantity of peanut butter sufficient for the sandwich, a butter knife or similar spreading utensil, and a plate or other flat work surface. Plain wheat or white sandwich bread is most commonly used. Peanut butter is available in smooth and crunchy varieties; the choice between them is a matter of preference and does not affect the procedure.

The two slices of bread are placed flat on the work surface. The peanut butter jar is opened. The butter knife is dipped into the peanut butter and a portion is scooped out. The exact quantity depends on personal preference; a typical sandwich uses one to two tablespoons of peanut butter, divided between the slices or applied entirely to one slice.

The peanut butter is spread on the chosen slice or slices. The spreading motion begins at the center of the bread and works outward toward the edges. Care is taken to cover the surface evenly, particularly the corners, which are otherwise dry when the sandwich is eaten. The bread should not be pressed hard during spreading, because peanut butter is sticky and dense and excessive pressure can tear the surface of the bread.

If two slices have been spread, they are placed peanut-butter sides together. If only one slice has been spread, the unspread slice is placed on top. The completed sandwich is pressed gently to seat the slices together.

The sandwich may be cut. Most sandwiches are cut diagonally from corner to corner, producing two triangular halves. Some preparers cut straight across the middle, producing two rectangular halves. Either is acceptable. Children's sandwiches are sometimes cut into four smaller pieces. The cutting is done with the butter knife or, for cleaner cuts, with a serrated bread knife.

The knife is wiped clean and the peanut butter jar is sealed. Any peanut butter remaining on the work surface is wiped up. The sandwich may be eaten immediately, wrapped in plastic or wax paper for later, or placed in a lunch container. Peanut butter sandwiches do not require refrigeration and remain palatable for many hours, which is part of their appeal as a portable lunch.
