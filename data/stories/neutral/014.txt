Boarding a commercial flight is a structured sequence of steps that begins well before passengers approach the aircraft and ends as the cabin door is closed. The procedure is largely standardized across major airlines, although local variations and aircraft type can introduce small differences.

Passengers arrive at the airport with a confirmed reservation, identification, and any required travel documents. They proceed to a check-in counter or self-service kiosk to receive a boarding pass and to deposit any checked luggage. Increasingly, boarding passes are issued electronically and stored on a mobile device. Checked luggage is weighed, tagged, and placed on a conveyor that carries it to the sorting facility.

Passengers then pass through the security screening point. Carry-on baggage is placed on a conveyor and scanned by an x-ray machine. Passengers walk through a metal detector or millimeter-wave scanner. Outerwear, belts, and electronic devices may be required to be removed and screened separately. Items prohibited from the cabin, such as containers of liquid exceeding the permitted volume, are surrendered or repacked into checked luggage.

After security, passengers proceed to the departure gate indicated on the boarding pass. The gate is identified by a letter and number corresponding to the terminal and position. Passengers wait in the gate area until boarding begins, typically thirty to forty-five minutes before scheduled departure.

Boarding proceeds in groups determined by the airline. Passengers requiring extra time and those traveling with small children are usually called first. Premium-class and frequent-flyer passengers board next, followed by the main cabin in groups defined by seat location, frequent-flyer status, or zone. The boarding pass is scanned at the gate; the passenger then walks down the jet bridge to the aircraft door.

Inside the aircraft, the passenger locates their seat by the number and letter printed on the boarding pass. Carry-on bags are stowed in the overhead bins; smaller personal items are placed under the seat in front. The passenger fastens the seat belt and remains seated unless a brief stretch is required.

When all passengers are aboard and counted, the cabin door is closed. The flight crew completes a final safety briefing, the aircraft is pushed back from the gate, and the engines are started. Taxi and departure follow according to instructions from air traffic control.
